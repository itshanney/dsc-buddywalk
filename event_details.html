The Down Syndrome Community is proud to welcome honored guest, Margaret-Lee Thompson as the Keynote Speaker for the 2016 Puget Sound Buddy Walk©. Margaret-Lee has recently retired as the Coordinator of the King County Parent Coalition for Developmental Disabilities, which is a program of The Arc of King County. The Parent Coalition was formed by Margaret-Lee in 1990 and is active in advocacy with the state legislature. Margaret-Lee's son, Dan Thompson, who passed away at the age of 36, was a featured speaker at the 1999 Buddy Walk and spoke of his work in the Microsoft mailroom and his love of the Mariners.

What better way is there to celebrate the 20th year of the Puget Sound Buddy Walk than to hear from two young people who have participated in the Buddy Walk for years?

Phillip Thelin, whose bagpipes have led the walk for many years, learned the art of piping from his grandfather, Bill Ross, who was the Walk's first piper, and then took over that role as a teen. Phillip is now employed in his dream job in Food Prep at Microsoft and will share his story with us.

Marcie Mineard has been a part of the Buddy Walk since she was a little girl, from leading the walk with her family in 1999, serving as a Greeter for many years, and then adding on sign language interpreting in 2014. Marcie has just celebrated a big milestone - her 10th anniversary at Safeway.

Master of Ceremonies Brian Calvert of KOMO News Radio has graciously agreed to be the Master of Ceremonies. He loved working with the Down Syndrome Community at the first Fashion Show and is back for more!
