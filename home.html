The Down Syndrome Community is proud to present the 20th Annual Puget Sound Buddy Walk® – a day of celebration to promote acceptance, inclusion and respect of individuals with Down syndrome.

Join over 1,500 friends and supporters for a day of fun including special 20th anniversary commemorations, activities for the family, live music, activities, food trucks, photos and of course, a walk around Seattle Center! Enjoy the entertainment, visit your favorite resource booths, and celebrate the talent of local artists with Down syndrome.

Your support and the support of our sponsors and friends helps the Down Syndrome Community (DSC) continue our ongoing efforts to improve the lives of individuals with Down syndrome and their families through community outreach, events, social groups, public education and advocacy.
Fundraising is optional but greatly appreciated! Donations can be made in honor of someone or just to help the DSC. Donations are tax deductible. All walk proceeds will support the DSC to foster acceptance and inclusion in the Puget Sound area.

Funds raised by the Puget Sound Buddy Walk® support the Down Syndrome Community's mission to improve the lives of individuals with Down syndrome and their families through:

  • The First Call Outreach Program, which provides up-to-date information, mentorship and support to parents who have received a Down syndrome diagnosis.
  • Community groups including DSC Friends which sponsors age-based social groups across the region, and D.A.D.S. of Puget Sound, which connects fathers of children with Down syndrome.
  • Community events like the annual Puget Sound Buddy Walk®, Sweetheart Dance & Pizza Party and the Big Time Talent Show.
  • Public education and advocacy, including the Learning Program, annual conferences and special guest speakers, which raise awareness of the issues affecting individuals with Down syndrome.
  • Self-advocacy initiatives that provide enrichment opportunities and employment resources as individuals with Down syndrome near adulthood.

The Buddy Walk® was developed by the National Down Syndrome Society (NDSS) in 1995 to celebrate Down Syndrome Awareness Month in October and to promote acceptance and inclusion of people with Down syndrome. The Buddy Walk® has grown from 17 walks in 1995 to nearly 300 Walks planned for 2015 worldwide. In 2014, nearly $13 million was raised nationwide to benefit local programs and services, as well as the national advocacy and public awareness initiatives of NDSS that benefit all individuals with Down syndrome.
